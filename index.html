The Problem

Why Ethereum Needed
Layer 2

Ethereum is powerful — but its base layer (Layer 1) has hard limits that create real pain for users and developers.

⚡

Congestion & Slow TPS

Ethereum mainnet processes only ~15–30 transactions per second. During peak demand (NFT drops, DeFi surges), the network jams — transactions take minutes or hours to confirm.

💸

Skyrocketing Gas Fees

Congestion triggers a gas fee bidding war. In 2021, average fees hit $50–$200 per transaction — making small-value transactions economically impossible for everyday users.

🚫

Accessibility Barrier

High costs lock out users in developing countries and micro-transaction use cases. DeFi, gaming, and NFT apps become impractical for mass adoption on mainnet alone.

The Solution

What is Arbitrum?

Arbitrum is an Optimistic Rollup Layer 2 network built on top of Ethereum. It bundles hundreds of transactions off-chain, compresses them, and posts a single proof back to Ethereum — inheriting its full security while achieving a fraction of the cost.

Built by Offchain Labs, Arbitrum is the largest Layer 2 by Total Value Locked (TVL), hosting major DeFi protocols like GMX, Uniswap, and Aave.

Real-World Impact

One Benefit That Changes Everything

🎮
Use Case: Blockchain Gaming

Micro-transactions at Virtually Zero Cost

On Ethereum mainnet, a game item purchase costs $15–$50 in gas alone — more than the item itself. On Arbitrum, that same transaction costs less than one cent.

This enables entirely new categories of applications: real-time in-game economies, micro-tipping, per-action NFT minting, and pay-per-use smart contracts — none of which are viable on mainnet.
