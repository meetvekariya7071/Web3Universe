Web2 vs Web3

The fundamental paradigm shift in how the internet works.

Web2
  • 🏢 Centralized servers
  • 🔑 Platform controls data
  • 👤 Accounts owned by companies
  • 💰 Revenue goes to platforms
  • 🚫 Censorship possible
  • 🔒 Walled gardens
VS
Web3
  • 🌐 Decentralized nodes
  • 🗝️ You own your data
  • 💎 Wallet-based identity
  • 💸 Creators earn directly
  • ✅ Censorship resistant
  • 🔓 Interoperable

VS

  • 🔧 World computer

Technology

Smart Contracts

Self-executing code that runs on the blockchain — no trust required.

Solidity 
contract SimpleEscrow {
  address buyer;
  address seller;
  uint amount;

  function release() public {
    require(msg.sender == buyer);
    seller.transfer(amount);
  }
}

Smart contracts are programs stored on the blockchain. They run when predetermined conditions are met — automating agreements without any trusted third party.

PoW vs PoS

The two dominant consensus mechanisms for validating blockchain transactions.

⛏️ Proof of Work
  • 🔢 Solve math puzzles
  • ⚡ Energy intensive
  • 💻 Hardware required
  • 🛡️ Battle-tested (BTC)
  • 🌡️ High heat output
  • 🌍 Large carbon footprint
VS
🌱 Proof of Stake
  • 💎 Lock up tokens
  • ♻️ 99.9% less energy
  • 💰 Capital required
  • 🔬 Newer, evolving (ETH)
  • ❄️ Low heat output
  • 🌿 Eco-friendly
Cryptography

Public Key vs Private Key

The cryptographic pair that makes trustless ownership possible.

🔓 Public Key
  • 📢 Shareable with anyone
  • 📥 Your wallet address
  • ✅ Used to verify signatures
  • 🔍 Visible on the blockchain
  • 📩 Others send funds to it
  • 🔑 Derived from private key
VS
🔐 Private Key
  • 🤫 Keep it absolutely secret
  • ✍️ Signs transactions
  • 👑 Proves you own funds
  • 🚪 Full wallet access
  • ❌ Lost = funds gone forever
  • 🎲 Random 256-bit number
💡

Think of it like email: Your public key is your email address — share it freely so people can send you funds. Your private key is your email password — never share it, or anyone can access and drain your wallet.

Blockchain vs Traditional Database

Two completely different philosophies for storing and trusting data.

🗄️ Traditional DB
  • 🏢 Controlled by one entity
  • ✏️ Records can be edited
  • 🗑️ Records can be deleted
  • 🔒 Requires trust in admin
  • ⚡ Very fast reads/writes
  • 💰 Low operational cost
VS
⛓️ Blockchain
  • 🌐 Distributed across nodes
  • 🔒 Immutable — append only
  • ♾️ Records never deleted
  • ✅ Trustless by design
  • 🐢 Slower — consensus needed
  • 💸 Higher per-TX cost
